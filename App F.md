<h1 id="appendix-f-dogcat-care">Appendix F: Dog/Cat Care</h1>
<h2 id="all">ALL</h2>
<ol>
<li>Inclusion criteria:
<ul>
<li>Dogs and cats ONLY</li>
<li>Dogs and cats encountered in the course of other emergency medical response.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="emt">EMT</h2>
<ol start="2">
<li>Protocol<br>
A. Ensure provider safety.  Utilize animal handler as necessary.<br>
B. Airway Management<br>
- Open and manually maintain airway if respiratory compromise suspected.<br>
- Administer supplemental oxygen as needed for suspected hypoxia.<br>
- Provide manual ventilation as needed by mouth-snout, mouth-barrier, or BVM.<br>
C. Hemorrhage management<br>
- Apply direct pressure as needed<br>
- Bandaging as needed.<br>
D. Fracture immobilization by standard methods, as needed.<br>
E. Naloxone - for suspected symptomatic opiate overdose<br>
- 0.04 mg/kg IN (dogs and cats)</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="medic">Medic</h2>
<pre><code>- 0.04 mg/kg IM/SC (dogs and cats)
</code></pre>
<h2 id="all-1">ALL</h2>
<p><strong>Notes:</strong><br>
A. Nothing in this protocol expands a providers scope of practice beyond that which is allowed in the care of human patients.</p>
<p>B. Providers utilizing this protocol should receive appropriate training in animal care techniques.</p>
