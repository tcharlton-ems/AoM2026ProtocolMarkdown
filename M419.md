<h1 id="sepsis">Sepsis</h1>
<h2 id="all">ALL</h2>
<p>I. <strong>Inclusion Criteria</strong></p>
<p>A. All ages</p>
<p>B. Provider suspects infection and</p>
<p>C. Adults: At least one (1) of the following abnormalities:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>SBP ≤ 90 mmHg</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>HR ≥ 90 bpm</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Visible tachypnea</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Acute altered mental status / confusion</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>D. Pediatrics: At least one (1) of the following abnormalities:</p>
<ol>
<li>Hypotension → a sign of uncompensated shock</li>
</ol>
<p>a. Neonates (0-28 days): SBP &lt; 60 mmHg</p>
<p>b. Infants (1 mo – 12 months): SBP &lt; 70 mmHg</p>
<p>c. Children (1 yr – 10 years): SBP &lt; 70 + (2 x age in years) mmHg</p>
<p>d. Children (&gt;10 years): SBP ≤ 90 mmHg</p>
<ol start="2">
<li>
<p>Sustained tachycardia for age</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Tachypnea for age</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Cool/pale/mottled skin</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Delayed capillary refill (&gt;2 seconds)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Altered mental status – sleepy, drowsy, fussy, irritable.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Weak peripheral pulses.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>In warm shock: flash capillary refill, bounding pulses.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>II. Protocol</p>
<p>A. Place patient on continuous ETCO2 monitor and record both the ETCO2 and measured respiratory rate.</p>
<p>B. Record temperature</p>
<p>C. If altered mental status, check fingerstick glucose and treat per M406 or P608.</p>
<p>III. <strong>Hospital Pre-Notification</strong></p>
<p>A. If the following criteria are met, pre-notify the receiving hospital with a “<strong>Sepsis Alert</strong>”:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>ETCO2 ≤ 25 and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>At least two (2) of the following:</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>a. T ≥ 100.4 F (38 C) OR ≤ 96.0 F (~36 C)</p>
<p>b. Hypotension</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Adults: SBP ≤ 90 mmHg</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Pediatric:</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>a. Neonates (0-28 days): SBP &lt; 60 mmHg</p>
<p>b. Infants (1 mo – 12 months): SBP &lt; 70 mmHg</p>
<p>c. Children (1 yr – 10 years): SBP &lt; 70 + (2 x age in years) mmHg</p>
<p>d. Children (&gt;10 years): SBP ≤ 90 mmHg</p>
<p>c. HR ≥ 90 bpm for adults; sustained tachycardia for age in pediatric patients (see chart above)</p>
<p>d. RR ≥ 20 bpm for adults; tachypnea for age in pediatric patients</p>
<p>e. Altered mental status / confusion</p>
<h2 id="medic">Medic</h2>
<p>IV. If “Sepsis Alert” criteria met:</p>
<p>A. Establish IV (or IO if indicated)</p>
<ol>
<li>Initiate IV fluids:</li>
</ol>
<p>a. Adult: (30 mL/kg crystalloid fluid; maximum of 500 milliliters) over less than 15 minutes.</p>
<p>b. Pediatric: (20mL/kg crystalloid fluid; using a push-pull method of drawing up the fluid in a syringe and pushing it through the IV (preferred for pediatric patients) - may repeat up to 3 times based on patient’s condition and clinical impression.</p>
<ol start="2">
<li>
<p>Do not delay transport to initiate IV/IO or fluid bolus.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>For persistent/worsening hypotension in non-pediatric patients, consider Push-Dose Epinephrine per SB205 Hypotension/Shock.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Most pediatric patients in the prehospital arena will need FLUIDS pushed/pulled and  have not been adequately fluid resuscitated to the point of needing pressors administered by a Paramedic.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="all-1">ALL</h2>
<p><strong>Notes:</strong></p>
<p>A. There are many disease processes that can cause abnormal vital signs. History and physical are important to inform your suspicion of an infection (inclusion criteria):</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Urinary: Indwelling catheter, history of UTI, urinary symptoms, etc.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Pulmonary: Cough, shortness of breath, aspiration, etc.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Bloodstream: IV drug use, wounds, indwelling lines, recent infections, etc.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Skin: Decubitus ulcer, diabetic wounds, cellulitis, etc.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>CNS: Confusion, seizures, photophobia, neck stiffness, etc.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Abdomen: Ascites with worsening abdominal pain or confusion, recent surgery, etc.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>B. When obtaining temperature, oral or rectal measurements are likely to be more accurate than superficial measurements, which often underestimate core temperature.</p>
<p>C. Any crystalloid fluid is appropriate for initial bolus (Normal Saline, Lactated Ringers, Normosol, Plasmalyte, etc.).</p>
