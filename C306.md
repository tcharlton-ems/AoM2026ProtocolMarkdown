<h1 id="narrow-complex-tachycardia-wpulse-unstable">Narrow Complex Tachycardia w/Pulse (Unstable)</h1>
<h2 id="all">ALL</h2>
<p><strong>I. Inclusion Criteria</strong></p>
<p>A. Patient’s age is 16 years and older.</p>
<p>B. No history of trauma or fever.</p>
<p>C. Pulse rate greater than 150.</p>
<p>D. Patient has signs of inadequate perfusion (for example: acute heart failure, delayed capillary refill, diaphoresis or altered mental status).</p>
<h2 id="medic">Medic</h2>
<p><strong>II. EKG Findings</strong></p>
<p>A. Rapid (greater than 150), regular atrial rate.</p>
<p>B. Normal QRS duration of less than 0.12 seconds.</p>
<p>C. P waves are usually absent.</p>
<h2 id="all-1">ALL</h2>
<p><strong>III. Protocol</strong></p>
<p>A. Assure airway patency and administer oxygen to correct hypoxia &lt;95%.</p>
<p>B. Place patient on cardiac monitor.</p>
<h2 id="emt">EMT</h2>
<p>C. If available, request ALS back-up or arrange to intercept an ALS unit as appropriate.</p>
<p>D. If no ALS available, initiate rapid transport to closest appropriate facility and provide pre- notification.</p>
<h2 id="medic-1">Medic</h2>
<p>E. Assess stability and if patient requires sedation prior to synchronized cardioversion consider following C305 Narrow Complex Tachycardia w/Pulse (Stable) Protocol</p>
<p>F. Do not delay Synchronized cardioversion for an unstable patient. Start with initial energy levels:</p>
<p>a. Narrow regular: 50-100 J;</p>
<p>b. Narrow irregular: 120-200 J biphasic or 200 J monophasic</p>
<p>G. If initial energy level fails, energy should be increased in a stepwise fashion from starting point for each subsequent shock: 100 J, 200 J, 300 J, and 360 J.</p>
<p>H. If the patient is to be cardioverted and does not have an altered level of consciousness, consider administer of Midazolam (Versed) until patient’s speech slurs.</p>

<p>A. Do not delay cardioversion if symptoms are severe.</p>
