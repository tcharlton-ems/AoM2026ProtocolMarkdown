<h1 id="cardiogenic-shock">Cardiogenic Shock</h1>
<h2 id="all">ALL</h2>
<p><strong>1. Inclusion Criteria</strong><br>
A. Patients age is 16 years or older.</p>
<p>B. The patient has chest pain suggestive of cardiac origin, dyspnea, no evidence of trauma, AND</p>
<p>C. Systolic blood pressure less than 80mm Hg supine, OR</p>
<p>D. Systolic blood pressure 80-100mm Hg and one of the following:</p>
<p>  1. Pulse greater than 120,</p>
<p>  2. Skin changes suggestive of shock, OR</p>
<p>  3. Altered mental status, agitation, or restlessness.</p>
<h2 id="medic">Medic</h2>
<p>II. <strong>Protocol</strong></p>
<p>A. Initiate large bore IV and administer 500ml normal saline fluid challenge if lungs are clear. If lungs are not clear, run IV at keep open rate. May repeat if lungs remain clear.</p>
<p>B. Consider Push dose epi per SB205 Hypotension. Multiple doses of fluid are preferred if the patient has an inferior MI.</p>
